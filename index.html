Attorney Pettway

After a family member was senselessly killed in a fire in October of 2003, it became essential to Attorney Pettway that her life, her career mean something. The idea of providing vigorous advocacy for those who were not skilled in navigating the legal system was born. Since 2004, she has zealously represented numerous clients with compassion for reasonable fees.

Attorney Pettway attended college at Duke University in Durham, North Carolina. At Duke, she majored in English and spent much of her spare time volunteering at various community service programs. She attended law school at Georgetown University Law Center in Washington, D.C. While in law school, she was on a law journal, worked with the Equal Justice Foundation, and received clinical training in the Juvenile Law Clinic. Currently, Attorney Pettway is a member of the Illinois Bar Association, Chicago Bar Association, the Northwest Suburban Association and Black Women Lawyers of Greater Chicago. She has served on judicial review panels, chaired the Chicago Bar Association Juvenile Law Committee, served on the Chicago Bar Association's Blue Ribbon Panel on the Detention Center, and sits on the Board of Directors for Maryville Academy.

Attorney Pettway handles a variety of legal matters, including but not limited to all areas of family law, criminal matters, election law, employment matters, and probate. Specializing in children's issues such as abuse and neglect matters, delinquency, and education law. She has acted as an arbitrator for Cook County hearing civil matters to assist in providing expedient resolution in various tort cases for the past eight years.
